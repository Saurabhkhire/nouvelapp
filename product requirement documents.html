Nouvel App — Product Requirements Document

1. Product Overview

Product Name: Nouvel
Founder: Nysa Chandna
Product Type: Mobile app for journaling, self-mastery, mindset development, emotional clarity, and personalized inner growth.

Nouvel is a guided self-development app designed to help users understand themselves more deeply, build emotional clarity, strengthen their mindset, and grow into the next version of themselves through personalized journaling, daily practices, AI-generated prompts, and self-mastery assessments.

The app should feel premium, calming, intentional, and personal — like a private digital sanctuary for reflection, alignment, and growth.

2. Core Product Goal

The goal of Nouvel is to create a personalized journaling and self-mastery experience where each user feels like the app understands their inner world, current season of life, emotional needs, and growth goals.

The app should help users:

  • Build self-awareness
  • Reflect on their thoughts and emotions
  • Understand what they desire in life
  • Shift their mindset and self-concept
  • Create stronger emotional regulation
  • Develop aligned daily habits
  • Receive personalized journaling prompts
  • Track their growth over time
  • Feel supported in becoming their highest version

3. Target Audience

Nouvel is designed for people who are growth-oriented, emotionally reflective, ambitious, spiritually curious, and interested in improving their mindset, self-concept, emotional balance, and life direction.

Primary users may include:

  • Women interested in personal growth and self-mastery
  • High achievers who want more emotional clarity
  • Creatives, founders, and entrepreneurs
  • People navigating identity shifts or life transitions
  • Users who enjoy journaling, mindfulness, spirituality, mindset work, and self-development
  • People who want personalized support without feeling overwhelmed

4. Brand Positioning

Nouvel is not just a journaling app. It is a guided space for inner mastery.

Brand essence:
A luxury self-mastery and journaling experience for people becoming the next version of themselves.

Core feeling:
Calm, elevated, emotionally intelligent, personal, celestial, feminine, and premium.

Possible tagline:
A guided space for self-mastery, clarity, and becoming.

5. Core User Flow

First-Time User Flow

  1. User opens app
  2. Welcome / brand intro screen
  3. Sign up or log in
  4. User enters basic profile information
  5. App introduces the self-discovery onboarding experience
  6. User answers personalized onboarding questions
  7. App analyzes answers
  8. App generates personalized results
  9. User sees their Top 3 Focus Areas
  10. User receives their first personalized journaling prompt
  11. User lands on home dashboard

Returning User Flow

  1. User opens app
  2. User lands on home dashboard
  3. User sees daily prompt, current focus area, and recommended practice
  4. User can journal, complete a practice, view progress, or ask AI for support
  5. User can revisit results or update their personal growth profile over time

7. Core Features

Feature 1: Personalized Onboarding Assessment

The user completes a 10-question assessment. Each answer maps to emotional themes and growth categories.

The app uses the assessment to generate:

  • Top 3 Focus Areas
  • Personalized journaling prompts
  • Daily practice recommendations
  • AI Guide tone and guidance style

Feature 2: Top 3 Focus Areas

The app should always generate exactly 3 focus areas from the assessment.

Preferred format:

  1. Focus Area Title
  2. Short personalized explanation
  3. Suggested direction for growth

Focus area examples:

  • Relationship Clarity & Emotional Boundaries
  • Deepening Peace Over Performance
  • Strengthening Aligned Communication
  • Protecting Nervous System Balance
  • Expanding Trust in Your Own Standards
  • Rebuilding Self-Worth from Within
  • Creating Consistency Through Self-Devotion
  • Clarifying Purpose & Direction
  • Strengthening Confidence in Identity
  • Releasing Overthinking & Emotional Loops

Feature 3: Personalized Journaling Prompts

The app generates prompts based on:

  • Assessment answers
  • Focus areas
  • User’s selected intention
  • Mood
  • Previous journal themes
  • User’s preferred support style

Prompt types:

  • Emotional clarity
  • Identity shift
  • Self-concept
  • Confidence
  • Boundaries
  • Relationships
  • Nervous system
  • Purpose
  • Abundance
  • Discipline
  • Self-trust

Feature 4: Journal Entries

Users can write and save journal entries.

Each entry should support:

  • Prompt text
  • User response
  • Date/time
  • Mood tracking
  • Tags/themes
  • Favorite/save option

Optional AI feature:

  • After saving, AI can generate a short reflection.

Example:
“Your entry shows a desire for more emotional honesty and stronger boundaries. A powerful next step may be naming what you need before trying to make others comfortable.”

Feature 5: Daily Practices

Users receive short practices based on their focus areas.

Examples:

  • 2-minute breathwork
  • 5-minute journal reflection
  • Boundary-setting exercise
  • Confidence affirmation
  • Nervous system reset
  • Gratitude practice
  • Identity scripting

Feature 6: AI Guide

The AI Guide acts as a personalized reflective companion.

It should be able to:

  • Ask deeper questions
  • Generate prompts
  • Reflect themes
  • Suggest practices
  • Help users clarify emotions
  • Recommend mindset shifts
  • Summarize progress

Tone should be:

  • Warm
  • Wise
  • Grounded
  • Emotionally safe
  • Premium
  • Clear
  • Encouraging without being overly casual

Feature 7: Progress Tracking

Users can see their growth and consistency.

Track:

  • Journal streak
  • Number of completed prompts
  • Practice completion
  • Mood before/after journaling
  • Weekly themes
  • Monthly reflection insights

Feature 8: Notifications

Push notifications can remind users to reflect.

Notification examples:

  • “Take a moment to return to yourself.”
  • “Your daily prompt is ready.”
  • “A few honest minutes with yourself can shift your whole day.”
  • “Pause. Breathe. Reflect.”

User should be able to set preferred reminder time.

8. AI Personalization Requirements

The AI system should use the user’s profile and assessment answers to personalize outputs.

Inputs AI Should Use

  • First name
  • Current life season
  • Main intention
  • Assessment answers
  • Top 3 Focus Areas
  • Mood check-ins
  • Journal history, if user consents
  • Completed practices
  • User’s preferred guidance style

AI Output Types

  • Top 3 Focus Areas
  • Personalized prompts
  • Reflection summaries
  • Daily practices
  • Weekly growth insights
  • Follow-up questions
  • Affirmations

AI Safety Boundaries

The AI must not:

  • Diagnose mental health conditions
  • Claim to replace therapy
  • Give medical advice
  • Give crisis intervention beyond encouraging emergency help or professional support
  • Make manipulative emotional claims
  • Store or expose private journal entries insecurely

14. Developer Notes

The app should be built with personalization as the main foundation. Even if the first version uses a simple scoring system instead of full AI, the experience should feel deeply personal.

The user should never feel like they are receiving generic prompts. Every result, prompt, and practice should feel connected to what they shared during onboarding.

Privacy is extremely important because users will be writing personal journal entries. Journal entries should be securely stored and never shared publicly.
